🛞 Tyres
Racing Soft = fastest lap times but wear out quickly (good for short stints or qualifying). Racing Hard = slower but last much longer. Match compound to race length and pit strategy.
▸ Common GR3 choice: Racing Medium or Hard for endurance
Lower pressure = bigger contact patch, more grip but more heat buildup. Higher = less rolling resistance, faster but less feedback. Too high = tyre sliding.
▸ GR3 default: ~29–31 psi
Same logic as front. Rear pressure also affects how the car rotates — lower rear pressure can help rotation into corners.
▸ GR3 default: ~29–31 psi
🛑 Brakes
Controls how much braking force goes to the front vs rear. Higher % = more front braking. If you lock front wheels under braking → reduce it. If rear slides on braking → increase it.
▸ GR3 default: 58%. Safe starting range: 55–62%
How aggressively the brakes respond to your input. Lower = softer, easier to modulate, less chance of lock-up. Higher = sharper, shorter braking zones but harder to control.
▸ GR3 default: 100%. New to a track? Try 90–95%
